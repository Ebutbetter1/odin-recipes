Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (Around 100°F)
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons instant or active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon brown or granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (melted and slightly cooled)
  • 3 3/4 cups all purpose flour
  • coarse salt/coarse sea salt
  • 1/2 cups baking soda
  • 9 cups water

Ingredients

  1. Whisk warm water, yeast, and sugar together in a large bowl. Cover the yeast mixture and let it sit for 5 minutes or until foamy on top.
  2. Add salt, melted butter, and 3 cups of flour. Beat on low speed (for stand mixer), then add 3/4 cup of flour. Continue to beat on low speed until the dough comes together and pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
  3. Knead the dough for 5 minutes. Ensure it slowly bounces back when poked or passes the 'windowpane test.'
  4. Cover with a towel and allow to rest for 10-30 minutes.
  5. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats.
  6. Cut the dough into 6 equal pieces. Roll each piece out into a 20-inch rope. Cut each rope into 1.5-2-inch pieces to make bites.
  7. Mix water and baking soda together in a large pot and bring to a boil. Place 8-10 bites into the water for 10-15 seconds. Lift them out and allow the excess water to drop off before placing on a baking sheet. Sprinkle each with coarse salt while they are still wet. Repeat with the remaining bites.
  8. Bake for 15 minutes or until golden brown. Serve while warm.
